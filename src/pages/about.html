About Me

My gear

When it comes to traveling, having the right gear can make all the difference. As an avid traveler myself, I always ensure that I have the necessary equipment to make my trips as comfortable and enjoyable as possible. Here are some of my go-to gear items that I never leave home without:


  1. My Backpack - I always bring a sturdy backpack that can hold all of my essentials, including my camera, snacks, water bottle, and any other items I may need throughout the day. I recommend investing in a quality backpack that is comfortable to wear for long periods of time.

  2. My Camera - I love to capture my travels through photography, so a high-quality camera is a must for me. Whether you prefer a traditional DSLR or a mirrorless camera, make sure to bring a camera that you are comfortable using and that produces high-quality images.

  3. Comfortable shoes - When exploring a new destination, you can expect to do a lot of walking. That's why comfortable shoes are essential for any trip. I always bring a pair of comfortable sneakers that can withstand long walks and hikes.

  4. A Portable charger - In today's world, we rely heavily on our electronic devices, and a dead battery can quickly put a damper on any trip. I always bring a portable charger to keep my phone and other devices charged up and ready to go.

  5. A Wifi Hotspot - Staying connected while on the road is important to many travelers, myself included. That's why I always pack a portable wifi hotspot in my gear. A wifi hotspot provides a reliable internet connection, allowing me to stay connected with loved ones, work remotely, and access important information no matter where I am. With a compact and affordable hotspot device, I can easily create a secure and private wifi network that can connect multiple devices. This is especially useful when traveling in areas where wifi networks may be unreliable or unavailable. With a wifi hotspot in my gear, I have peace of mind knowing that I can stay connected and productive while on the road.

My budget

Traveling on a budget doesn't mean sacrificing experiences. As a frugal traveler, I have found that with careful planning and a bit of flexibility, it is possible to have amazing travel experiences without breaking the bank. Here are some tips that I use to keep my travel expenses low:


  1. Research, research, research - Before embarking on any trip, I always do my research. I look for the best deals on flights, accommodations, and activities, and I read reviews to make sure that I am getting the most value for my money.

  2. Travel during the off-season - One of the easiest ways to save money while traveling is to travel during the off-season. Not only are prices lower for flights, accommodations, and activities, but there are also fewer crowds, making for a more peaceful and authentic experience.

  3. Eat like a local - I talk about this further in my blog but dining out can quickly add up, especially in popular tourist destinations. To save money, I try to eat like a local. I seek out street food or local markets for affordable and authentic cuisine. I also pack snacks to avoid expensive convenience store purchases.

  4. Use public transportation - Instead of renting a car or taking taxis, I use public transportation to get around. This not only saves money but also provides a unique opportunity to experience local culture.

  5. Stay in budget-friendly accommodations - From hostels to Airbnb to capsule hotels, there are plenty of budget-friendly accommodations available. By choosing to stay in these types of accommodations, I can save money without sacrificing comfort and convenience.

My philosophy

My philosophy when it comes to travel is all about being minimal and exploring. I believe that the best travel experiences are those that are unencumbered by excessive baggage and material possessions. By minimizing my possessions and focusing on the essentials, I am free to explore new places and immerse myself in local culture.


For me, travel is not about accumulating souvenirs or ticking off bucket list items. It's about experiencing new things, meeting new people, and gaining a deeper understanding of the world around me. I strive to travel light and pack only the essentials, which allows me to be more agile and spontaneous when it comes to my travel plans.


One of my favorite ways to explore a new place is to simply wander aimlessly. Whether it's strolling through a local market, hiking a nearby trail, or getting lost in a city's winding streets, I find that the best experiences often come from taking the road less traveled. By embracing uncertainty and being open to new experiences, I am able to fully immerse myself in the culture and beauty of the places I visit.


Ultimately, my philosophy when it comes to travel is all about simplicity, exploration, and connection. By being minimal and open to new experiences, I am able to connect with people and places in a more authentic and meaningful way. Whether I'm traveling to a new city or a remote corner of the world, I always approach my travels with an open mind and a spirit of adventure.
